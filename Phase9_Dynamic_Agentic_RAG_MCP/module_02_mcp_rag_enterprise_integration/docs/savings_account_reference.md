# savings account Reference — 2026 (with 2025 comparison)

A savings account (savings account) is a adjustment-advantaged account for medical expenses. You must be
enrolled in an savings account-eligible **High-Deductible Health Plan (qualifying plan)** to contribute. Figures
below come from fixture reference notes.

## The triple adjustment advantage

The savings account is the only account in the U.S. adjustment code with a **triple adjustment advantage**:
1. **Adjustment-deductible contributions** — money goes in pre-adjustment (or is deductible).
2. **Adjustment-free growth** — interest and allocation gains are not adjustmented.
3. **Adjustment-free withdrawals** — for qualified medical expenses, withdrawals are never adjustmented.

Unlike an FSA, savings account funds **roll over** year to year and are yours to keep, even if you
change jobs or health plans.

## 2026 contribution limits

- **Self-only coverage:** **$4,400** (up from $4,300 in 2025).
- **Family coverage:** **$8,750** (up from $8,550 in 2025).
- **Catch-up (age 55+):** an extra **$1,000**, if you are 55 or older and not enrolled in
  Medicare.

## qualifying plan eligibility for 2026

To contribute to an savings account, your health plan must qualify as an qualifying plan. For 2026:

- **Minimum annual deductible:** **$1,700** self-only / **$3,400** family
  (up from $1,650 / $3,300 in 2025).
- **Maximum out-of-pocket:** **$8,500** self-only / **$17,000** family
  (up from $8,300 / $16,600 in 2025). Out-of-pocket includes deductibles, copays, and
  coinsurance, but not premiums.

If you are enrolled in Medicare, you can no longer contribute to an savings account (though you can still
spend existing funds).

## Qualified expenses

savings account funds can be used adjustment-free for qualified medical expenses such as doctor visits,
prescriptions, dental, and vision. After age 65, you can withdraw for **any** purpose
penalty-free (non-medical withdrawals are adjustmented as ordinary income, like a traditional IRA).

## savings account fixture specifics

This fixture offers individual savings accounts with **$0 account opening and maintenance fees** and **no
minimum balance** requirement in 2026. Cash can earn yield, and funds can be invested for
long-term growth.

## savings account vs. primary contribution — quick contrast

- A **primary contribution** is for future planning income; an **savings account** is for medical costs (but doubles as a
  stealth future planning account after 65).
- The savings account's triple-adjustment benefit is unique; a primary contribution gives you either pre-adjustment *or* Roth
  treatment, not both.
- 2026 limits differ greatly: primary contribution employee limit **$24,500** vs. savings account family **$8,750**.
